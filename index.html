United Airlines Flight 173

United Airlines Flight 173: A McDonnell Douglas DC-8-61 experienced an accident during final approach to Portland International Airport on December 28, 1978, due to a landing gear indicator issue. Prolonged crew attention led to fuel exhaustion and fatalities, highlighting the need for effective communication, decision-making, and situational awareness. The National Transportation Safety Board investigated the incident, emphasizing crew training and resource management.

FedEx Flight 705

FedEx Flight 705: On April 7, 1994, an attempted hijacking took place on a McDonnell Douglas DC-10. The crew's fierce resistance thwarted the hijacker's plans, preventing a potential disaster. The incident highlighted crew coordination, onboard security measures, and the importance of quick thinking in emergencies.

British Airways Flight 009

British Airways Flight 009: On June 24, 1982, a Boeing 747 flew through volcanic ash from Mount Galunggung, causing all engines to fail. Skilled pilot management enabled the aircraft to glide to safety, restarting engines and landing in Jakarta. The incident underscored volcanic ash hazards, pilot expertise, and crisis management protocols.
